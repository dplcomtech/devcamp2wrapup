About FFRC

History, Funders, and Opportunities

History

The neighborhoods surrounding Lowry Air Force Base took an economic downturn after the base closed in 1993. In response, the Lowry Family Center, as part of the Community College of Aurora, opened in March 1994 to support families in those neighborhoods with case management, family advocacy, counseling services, parenting, and women's support groups.

Over the years, LFC saw a growing need for its services outside of the increasingly affluent Lowry neighborhood. To meet that need, LFC moved its main office to Montbello in 2005, while maintaining a smaller satellite office in north Aurora near Lowry. Lowry Family Center has expanded to now serve all of "far" northeast Denver and north Aurora.

For a variety of reasons, including a need to become financially stable and independent, LFC left the community college and became a project of the Colorado Nonprofit Development Center (CNDC) on October 1, 2005. LFC is one of 24 member agencies of the Colorado Family Resource Center Association (FRCA).

We have recently changed our name from Lowry Resource Center to Families Forward Resource Center (FFRC). This was a huge undertaking and we are happy to announce our name change. The change will address the location based confusion many experience when they realize we grown into the communities of Montbello, Green Valley Ranch and north Aurora. Thank you for your support and we hope you'll continue to support Families Forward Resource Center.

Get Involved

Be a Volunteer

FFRC is looking for a fleet of dedicated individuals to help with the increasing demand for community engagement efforts in Montbello, Green Valley Ranch, and northern Aurora. We are excited to work with volunteers that have an interest in the communities we server and in strengthening families. There are various volunteer opportunities in programs, development, and administration. Please see the other opportunities below, and fill out the submission form if interested in volunteering for FFRC.

Programs

Community Health Volunteer: This individual will report to our Health Advocate, Veronica Armas. Duties include, but are not limited to: Attending outreach events, recruiting individuals for health classes, developing partnerships with other agencies, assisting with public benefits applications, and sitting on internal and external health committees.

Fatherhood Volunteer: Calling fathers to join the discussion on what is means to be a father and help FFRC's Fatherhood Coordinator, Ron Allen, by assisting with the following duties: recruiting men into the fatherhood program, calling and encouraging fathers to continue their mentoring, participating in various community events involving fatherhood and strengthening families.

Kinship Volunteer: FFRC's Kinship Advocate, Felicia Allen, is looking for an individual who is familiar with the world of kinship and foster parenting. This individual will help with the following duties: resource research, Kinship/Foster Parent's Night Out, receptionist duties like filing, copying, phones and assisting with the 'Solutions Group' aspect of the kinship program.

Youth Development Volunteer: These individuals will work closely with Family Advocate, Jerry Burks. FFRC is currently looking for individuals that would like join the Summer Camp "Kathy's Kamp" parent planning committee. This committee is open to parents that have children that attend Kathy's Kamp and individuals that may have a vested interest in keeping kids active and off the streets during the summer. We are also looking for volunteers interested in developing our Youth Leadership Squad which serves youth 14-18yrs.

General Program Support Volunteer: Year round program support is very much needed at FFRC. We have extensive Thanksgiving and Christmas programs that require extra hands in addition to a busy summer of outreach events where we would like to be present. Duties include but are not limited to: cold calling, office support, organizing and filing, attending outreach events, and volunteering for specific FFRC community engagement programs.

Development

Development Volunteer: This individual will work with the development team and the Director of Development, La Toya Petty. Duties include: event planning, volunteering the day of major events, recruitment of additional volunteers, translations (English/Spanish), Fundraising and Campaign assistance in addition to community engagement in funding opportunities.

Administration

Office Assistant Volunteer: The Office Assistant Volunteer will work closely with the Director of Operations, Sandy Winston and the individuals housed at the Montbello office. This individual will help with incoming traffic, phone and front desk duties, and office upkeep.
